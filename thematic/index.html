Dendrimer-Mediated
Columnar Mesophase of Surfactants

Columnar mesophase, in which the molecular or supramolecular building blocks with rod-like geometry pack in two-dimensional (2D) lattices, is an important class of mesomorphic structure having been found in various liquid crystalline materials for practical applications.

We demonstrated the power of dendrimer in tuning the long-range ordered packing of oppositely charged cylinders to yield a rich structural polymorphism of columnar mesophase that may be exploited for the development of functional materials.

Dendrimers

Dendrimers are unique tree-like hyperbranched macromolecules of well-defined nanostructure and nearly monodispersed size.
The tunable functionality and the similarity of molecular size to that of certain globule proteins such as histone octamer make dendrimers a promising material for biomedical applications.

Self-Assembly of Polymers

Self-assembly using molecular building blocks offers a facile and effective approach to create functional materials with well-defined and controllable structures.
